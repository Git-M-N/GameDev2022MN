Asian economy in the 80s

In the 1980s, the Asian economy experienced a period of rapid growth and development. This period saw the emergence of several newly industrialized countries (NICs) in the region, including South Korea, Taiwan, Hong Kong, and Singapore. These countries experienced high growth rates, low inflation, and increased trade and investment flows. These NICs, along with Japan, formed the basis of the so-called Asian miracle, which saw rapid economic growth and development in the region. In addition, several other countries in the region also experienced strong economic growth, including Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. This period of rapid growth was largely driven by export-led growth, as well as significant foreign investment from countries such as the United States and Japan.
