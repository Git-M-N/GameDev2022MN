Taiwan China chip conflict

Taiwan is one of the world’s largest producers of semiconductor chips, or integrated circuits, used in a wide range of consumer electronics and other products. In recent years, the island has become increasingly vital to the global chip industry, with some of the world’s largest chipmakers, such as Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co. (TSMC), located there. However, the island’s chip production has come under increasing pressure in recent months, due to the ongoing trade war between the United States and China, as well as tensions between China and Taiwan. As a result, the US has imposed restrictions on the sale of US-made chipmaking equipment to Chinese companies, while China has threatened to restrict the sale of chips made in Taiwan to Chinese companies. These developments have put chipmakers in Taiwan in a difficult position, as they must now choose between continuing to supply Chinese customers and risking US sanctions, or cutting off their Chinese customers and risking losing a significant portion of their revenue.
