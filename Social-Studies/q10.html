India political direction

India is a federal parliamentary democratic republic governed by a multi-party system. The government is led by the Prime Minister, who is the head of government, and the President, who is the head of state. India's Constitution guarantees citizens a wide range of civil and political rights, including freedom of speech, freedom of association, and the right to vote. India has a diverse and growing economy and boasts a large population of 1.3 billion people. India is a rising global power and has become an influential actor on the world stage. Its economy is the sixth-largest in the world and is projected to grow further in the coming years. India is committed to the principles of democracy, pluralism, secularism, and the rule of law. India has a strong commitment to international cooperation and is a major contributor to global peace and security. India is committed to the Sustainable Development Goals and is making significant progress towards achieving them. India is a major player in regional and global affairs, with an expanding role in international institutions such as the United Nations, G20, and BRICS.
