North-Korea Economy

North Korea's economy is largely isolated from the rest of the world. It is a centrally-planned economy dominated by the state-owned enterprises. The primary industries in North Korea are agriculture, mining, and manufacturing. The agricultural sector is the biggest employer, accounting for almost 40% of the population. Mining and manufacturing are also important, but only account for about 10 to 15% of the economy. The government heavily subsidizes the price of food and energy, and has also implemented a number of economic reforms in recent years. North Korea does have some foreign trade, mostly with China, but it is heavily restricted by international sanctions. The North Korean economy is struggling and the country faces chronic food shortages and high levels of poverty.
