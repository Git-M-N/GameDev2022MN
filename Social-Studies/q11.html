Japan political direction

Japan has experienced a steady shift in its political direction since the end of World War II. After decades of conservative rule, the Liberal Democratic Party (LDP) was voted out of office in 2009, making way for the Democratic Party of Japan (DPJ). This resulted in a move away from the country's traditional economic policy of close government control in favor of more free-market reforms. The DPJ was voted out of office in 2012, and the LDP returned to power. Since then, Japan has experienced a shift towards an even more conservative direction under Prime Minister Abe Shinzo, who has focused on increasing defense spending, amending the pacifist constitution, and strengthening ties with the US. Despite this shift in political direction, Japan has continued to maintain its close economic ties with the US and other Asian countries, while also nurturing a strong relationship with China.
